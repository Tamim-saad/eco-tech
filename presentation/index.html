② Proposed Solution

Introducing NodiWatch

An AI-powered satellite surveillance platform that transforms 10 years of free satellite imagery into actionable enforcement intelligence for Bangladesh's rivers.

🏭 Pollution Fingerprinting (নদী দূষণ)

Multispectral indices (NDTI, CDOM, SWIR color ratios) profile effluent type — textile dye (Red/Blue ratio), tannery (high turbidity + organic load), chemical discharge (SWIR anomalies). Each industrial cluster gets a probability profile.

🏗️ Encroachment Detection (নদী দখল)

MNDWI-based water segmentation compares 2016 vs 2026 river boundaries from Sentinel-2 imagery (10m resolution). Automatically generates enforcement-grade intelligence showing river width shrinkage over time.

🌊 Erosion Risk Forecasting (নদী ভাঙন)

Sentinel-1 SAR radar (works through monsoon clouds, 12-day revisit) + temporal analysis tracks shoreline regression. AI forecasts probabilistic erosion risk corridors — enabling BWDB and DDM to deploy resources proactively.

River pollution visible from satellite — NodiWatch profiles the effluent type & likely source cluster

💡 Key Insight: The same AI that detects land filling (encroachment) also detects land disappearing (erosion). One model, two critical problems solved.

③ AI / Modern Technology

How Our AI Actually Works

STEP 1

Water Segmentation (MNDWI + ML)

Modified Normalized Difference Water Index (MNDWI) classifies every pixel as water or land. By comparing 2016 vs 2026 water masks, we detect where river area shrank (encroachment) or where land disappeared (erosion). ML enhances boundary accuracy.

STEP 2

Pollution Classification (Random Forest)

A Random Forest model learns unique spectral signatures: textile effluent = high Red/Blue ratio, tannery = high turbidity (NDTI) + organic load (CDOM), chemical discharge = SWIR anomalies. Each polluted zone gets classified by effluent type.

STEP 3

Erosion Forecasting (SAR + ML)

Sentinel-1 SAR penetrates monsoon clouds (12-day cycle). Combined with ML, it tracks shoreline retreat rate and forecasts high-risk erosion corridors for the next season — even during heavy rains when optical satellites are blind.

STEP 4

Industrial Cluster Profiling (Bayesian)

For each pollution hotspot, the system queries OpenStreetMap for factories within 500m. A Bayesian model weighs distance + pollution pattern + industry type to rank suspects.

Example output:
"Textile cluster: 78% • Tannery cluster: 22%"

🎯 Why This Architecture?

• MNDWI = proven, robust water-land classification index
• Random Forest = fast, interpretable spectral matching
• SAR = weather-independent, critical for monsoon-heavy Bangladesh
• Bayesian = rigorous probabilistic ranking with spatial decay
